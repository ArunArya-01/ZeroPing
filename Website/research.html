Research

Cutting-edge aerospace research and development

Abstract

This research presents a novel hybrid approach that combines traditional physics-based modeling with deep learning techniques to address the limitations of existing aerodynamic prediction methods. Our work focuses on capturing the discrepancies between textbook physics and real-world flight behavior.

The Problem

Existing aerodynamic models struggle to accurately predict fuel burn and performance under varying flight conditions, particularly during climb profiles and at high altitudes. Traditional methods fail to account for complex interactions between aircraft systems and atmospheric conditions.

Our Approach

We propose a hybrid architecture that integrates physics-based models with machine learning. The loss function is designed to enforce physics consistency while learning from real flight data:

\mathcal{L} = \lambda_1 \mathcal{L}_{physics} + \lambda_2 \mathcal{L}_{data} + \lambda_3 \mathcal{L}_{consistency}

Key Findings

  • Hybrid model achieves 15-20% better accuracy than pure physics models
  • ML components capture non-linear aerodynamic effects missed by traditional methods
  • Physics consistency constraints prevent overfitting to training data
  • Significant improvement in fuel burn prediction under unusual flight conditions
